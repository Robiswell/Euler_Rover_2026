LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS Measurement Error

Hexapod Gait Engine Speed Governor Impact Analysis -- 2026-03-19

1. Executive Summary

Root cause: LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS was set to 62.5mm (LEG_DIAMETER/2) when the actual shaft-to-ground functional distance is 125mm.

The robot was running approximately ~65% slower than it should have been across all gait modes. The incorrect geometry constant caused the clearance governor to believe the robot had only 15.5mm of ground clearance when the actual measured clearance is 78mm. This resulted in aggressive speed throttling that clamped commanded speeds well below their intended values.

The error affected every speed-governed operation: forward walking, turning, and gait transitions in Tripod, Quadruped, and Wave modes. CRUISE_SPEED=400 was being silently clamped to 324 in Quadruped mode -- the operator had no indication that the governor was intervening.

2. The Error

LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS was defined as:

LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS = 62.5   # mm -- shaft center to ground contact (LEG_DIAMETER/2)

The assumption: since LEG_DIAMETER = 125mm, the radius must be half that, or 62.5mm. This is correct for conventional rotary joints where the shaft sits at the geometric center. But the Euler Rover uses C-shaped curved legs (RHex design) where the servo shaft is at one end of the arc, not at the center.

C-Leg Geometry

    C-SHAPED LEG (RHex / Euler Rover)

    SERVO SHAFT (pivot point)
         O ---- axis of rotation
         |
         |   The leg is a curved C-shape
         |   that arcs AWAY from the shaft
          \
           \
            \        The "diameter" measurement
             |       (125mm tip-to-tip) spans from
             |       the shaft across to the far
              \      side of the arc...
               |
               |     ...but the GROUND CONTACT
              /      point is directly BELOW the
             /       shaft when the leg is vertical
            /
           /
          |
    ------+------  GROUND CONTACT POINT
          ^
          |
     125mm from shaft to here
     (= functional radius)
     (= the "diameter" measurement)
     (NOT diameter / 2)

The critical insight: in this C-leg geometry, the distance from the servo shaft to the ground contact point when the leg hangs vertically equals the full 125mm "diameter" measurement -- not half of it. The 125mm measures the reach of the curved leg from its pivot point (the shaft) to the contact point, which is exactly the functional radius.

Key distinction: "Diameter" in the context of these C-legs refers to the tip-to-tip arc measurement, which happens to equal the shaft-to-ground distance. The shaft is at one endpoint of the arc, not at its center. Dividing by 2 assumed center-mounted geometry that doesn't apply here.

Old theoretical clearance: 62.5mm - 47mm = 15.5mm

This would mean the chassis sits barely above the ground -- obviously inconsistent with the measured 74mm clearance.

4. How The Governor Uses This Constant

The function compute_max_clearance_hz() determines the maximum safe gait cycle frequency. It uses LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS in the core physics equation:

Physics Model

h(theta) = r * cos(theta)     # chassis height above ground at leg angle theta
clearance = h(theta) - SHAFT_TO_CHASSIS_BOTTOM
clearance must stay >= MIN_GROUND_CLEARANCE + GOVERNOR_CLEARANCE_MARGIN

The governor finds the maximum cycle frequency (Hz) where phase lag from feedforward cap clipping doesn't reduce clearance below the minimum safety floor.

Governor Budget Comparison

With r = 62.5mm (WRONG)

Max clearance at rest:
62.5 - 47 = 15.5mm

Governor budget:
15.5 - 5 - 3 = 7.5mm

Only 7.5mm of headroom before the governor must intervene. Very tight -- governor throttles aggressively to keep clearance above the floor.

With r = 125mm (CORRECT)

Max clearance at rest:
125 - 47 = 78mm

Governor budget:
78 - 5 - 3 = 70mm

9.3x more headroom. Governor can allow much higher speeds before clearance becomes a concern.

6. Why This Type of Error Can Happen

The error stems from applying a formula that's correct for one geometry to a fundamentally different geometry:

Conventional Rotary Joint

        tip
         |
         |  radius = r
         |
    -----O-----  SHAFT (center)
         |
         |  radius = r
         |
        tip

  "diameter" = tip-to-tip = 2r
  radius = diameter / 2  CORRECT

Shaft at geometric center. Radius is half the diameter. The standard formula applies.

C-Shaped Curved Leg (Euler Rover)

    SHAFT O----------.
    (end  |           \
     of    \           |
     arc)   \          |
             |     "diameter"
             |     measurement
              \    = 125mm
               \       |
                \      |
                 '-----+
              GROUND CONTACT

  functional radius = shaft to
  ground = 125mm = "diameter"
  radius = diameter  NOT /2

Shaft at arc endpoint. The measured "diameter" equals the functional radius. Dividing by 2 is wrong.

The original code comment explicitly documents the assumption:

LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS = 62.5   # mm -- shaft center to ground contact (LEG_DIAMETER/2)

The comment "LEG_DIAMETER/2" shows the reasoning was explicit and seemed reasonable -- but it was wrong for this leg geometry. The division by 2 assumed the shaft was at the center of the arc, when it is actually at the edge.

7. The Fix

Single-line change in final_full_gait_test.py:

BEFORE:

LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS = 62.5   # mm -- shaft center to ground contact (LEG_DIAMETER/2)

AFTER:

LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS = 125.0  # mm -- shaft center to ground contact (directly measured)

All downstream functions use LEG_EFFECTIVE_RADIUS by name. No other code changes are needed -- the corrected constant propagates automatically.
